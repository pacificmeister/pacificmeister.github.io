What Is an Electric Surfboard?

"Electric surfboard" is an umbrella term for any board-shaped watercraft powered by an electric motor and rechargeable battery. The category has exploded since the mid-2010s, and what started as a niche DIY experiment (hi, that was me) is now a global industry with dozens of brands and several distinct product types.

Here's the thing most "best electric surfboard" articles won't tell you: these products are fundamentally different from each other. An eFoil and a jetboard share about as much DNA as a mountain bike and a motorcycle — yes, they both have wheels, but the experience is completely different.

The common thread: a lithium battery (usually 1–3 kWh), a brushless motor (jet drive or propeller), a wireless hand controller, and a board to stand on. Beyond that, they diverge wildly in how they move through water, how they feel to ride, who they're for, and what they cost.

Let's break down every type so you can figure out which one actually matches what you're looking for.

The 5 Types of Electric Surfboards

🦅

1. eFoil (Electric Hydrofoil Surfboard)

What it is: A surfboard with an underwater wing (hydrofoil) and electric motor mounted on a mast below the board. At speed, the hydrofoil generates lift and the board rises completely out of the water, creating a silent, gliding flight sensation 1–3 feet above the surface.

Price Range
$6,000–$16,000
Top Speed
25–35 mph
Ride Time
60–120 min
Learning Curve
Moderate

✅ Pros

  • Nearly silent — rides above chop and wake
  • Most efficient (less drag = longer battery life)
  • Unique "flying" sensation unlike anything else
  • Usable on flat water, rivers, lakes, ocean
  • Strong DIY community and open-source designs

❌ Cons

  • Steeper learning curve (2–5 sessions to fly)
  • Hydrofoil can be dangerous if handled carelessly
  • Expensive replacement parts (mast, foil wings)
  • Not great for adrenaline junkies wanting raw speed
  • Requires care when launching/landing

Who it's for: Riders who want a unique, meditative flying experience. Works on any body of water — flat lakes, rivers, ocean. The most versatile electric watercraft and the one most people become addicted to after trying. If you've ever dreamed of flying, this is the closest you'll get on water.

⭐ Top picks: Lift5 (best overall, $14K), Waydoo Flyer ONE Plus (best value, $6.3K), DIY build (best bang-for-buck, $2–4.5K)
🏄‍♂️

2. Electric Jetboard (Surface-Riding)

What it is: A motorized surfboard that rides on the water surface, powered by a jet drive or propeller. Think of it as a surfboard with a built-in engine — you stand on it and cruise across the water just like surfing, but without needing waves or a tow rope.

Price Range
$4,000–$14,000
Top Speed
25–37 mph
Ride Time
25–50 min
Learning Curve
Easy–Moderate

✅ Pros

  • Easiest to ride — familiar surfboard stance
  • Higher top speed potential
  • More intuitive for surfers/wakeboarders
  • Jet drive models are very safe (no exposed prop)
  • Great for speed runs and carving

❌ Cons

  • Shorter battery life (surface drag is high)
  • Rough in choppy water — you feel every wave
  • Louder than eFoils
  • Less unique sensation (like motorized surfing)
  • Limited to conditions with minimal chop

Who it's for: Speed lovers, surfers, and wakeboarders who want a powered version of what they already do. Easiest to learn if you have any board sports background. Best in calm water — lakes, bays, flat ocean days.

⭐ Top picks: Awake RÄVIK 3 ($11K, premium performance), Radinn Freeride ($4.5K, entry-level), Awake RÄVIK S ($5K, best mid-range)
⛽

3. Gas-Powered Jetboard (JetSurf)

What it is: A motorized surfboard powered by a small internal combustion engine (ICE). JetSurf essentially created and dominates this category. These are the fastest motorized surfboards available and have an international racing circuit.

Price Range
$8,000–$14,000
Top Speed
37–40+ mph
Ride Time
40–60 min
Learning Curve
Moderate

✅ Pros

  • Fastest motorized surfboard category
  • Quick refuel (no 2+ hour charge wait)
  • Established racing community & events
  • Proven reliability (10+ year track record)
  • No battery degradation over time

❌ Cons

  • Loud — engine noise, exhaust
  • Requires fuel mixing and engine maintenance
  • Banned on many lakes and waterways
  • Heavier than electric equivalents
  • Not the "silent glide" experience most buyers want

Who it's for: Speed addicts and competitive riders who want the fastest possible motorized surfboard and don't mind the noise. The established racing circuit makes this attractive for competitive types. Not ideal if you want a peaceful water experience or ride on noise-restricted waterways.

⭐ Top pick: JetSurf Titanium ($13K) for performance, JetSurf Adventure ($8.5K) for recreational use. JetSurf also makes an electric model now.
🐬

4. Electric Bodyboard / Boogie Board

What it is: A smaller, prone-riding (lying down) electric watercraft. Think of a bodyboard with a built-in jet drive. These are the most affordable entry point into electric water sports and the safest for kids and absolute beginners.

Price Range
$1,500–$5,000
Top Speed
10–20 mph
Ride Time
30–60 min
Learning Curve
Very Easy

✅ Pros

  • Lowest cost entry point
  • Virtually no learning curve — lie down and go
  • Safest option (low to water, low speed)
  • Great for kids and families
  • Compact, easy to transport

❌ Cons

  • Limited speed and range
  • Prone position only (no standing)
  • Gets boring quickly for adults
  • Not a "surfboard" experience
  • Less established market (fewer options)

Who it's for: Families, kids, resort/rental operators, and anyone who wants the simplest possible electric water experience. Great as a gateway toy, but most adults outgrow them quickly and upgrade to an eFoil or jetboard.

⭐ Top picks: Lift Blowfish (converts Lift eFoil to stable platform), SiFly Boardjet ($3K), various Chinese imports on Amazon ($1.5–2.5K, quality varies hugely)
🚀

5. Electric Tow Boogie / Tow Device

What it is: Not technically a surfboard — a tow boogie is a motorized tow device that pulls you up onto a foil, then releases so you ride waves or downwind bumps on an unpowered prone foilboard. Think of it as a personal jet ski replacement for foil surfers — it gets you up to speed, then you fly on pure wave energy.

For a deeper engineering breakdown, read our tow boogie hull design guide (rocker, rails, tow-point geometry, and prototype testing).

Price Range
$2,000–$8,500
Top Speed
10–15 mph
Ride Time
60–120+ min
Learning Curve
Expert

✅ Pros

  • Enables solo foil surfing without a jet ski
  • Ride pure wave energy (most authentic feel)
  • Very long battery life (short tow-ups, long glides)
  • Growing DIY community and open designs
  • No motor drag while riding waves

❌ Cons

  • Requires existing foiling skills
  • Not a standalone product (needs separate foilboard)
  • Very niche market — few commercial options
  • Needs ocean swell or downwind bumps
  • Emerging category — tech still maturing

Who it's for: Experienced foil surfers who want to catch more waves without a jet ski or boat. This is the most "core" option — it's not for beginners, but for skilled foilers it's the holy grail. The DIY build community is growing rapidly.

⭐ Top picks: Zero Tow V3 ($8.3K, market leader), Foil Fusion ($4K, US-made), DIY build ($750–$1,200)

💡 The efficiency gap is real. eFoils fly above the water, eliminating surface drag — that's why they last 60–120 minutes while jetboards (fighting drag the whole time) only get 25–50 minutes from similar battery packs. If ride time matters to you, this is the single biggest differentiator.

Which Type Is Right for You?

🆕 "I've never ridden any board sport"

Start with: Electric bodyboard (safest, cheapest) or eFoil with a large stable board and flight assist. The bodyboard gets you on the water immediately with zero learning curve. If you want to progress, an eFoil with flight-assist technology (Waydoo, Lift, Fliteboard) dramatically shortens the learning curve.

🏄 "I surf/wakeboard and want something motorized"

Best match: Electric jetboard. Your existing balance and carving skills transfer directly. The sensation is closest to what you already know — it's surfing with an engine. Try an eFoil too though; many surfers try it "just once" and never go back to surface riding.

🧘 "I want a peaceful, unique experience on the water"

Best match: eFoil, hands down. Nothing else feels like silently flying above the water at sunrise. The meditative quality of eFoiling is what hooks most riders. It's not about speed — it's about the sensation.

🏎️ "I want the fastest thing possible"

Best match: Gas JetSurf (40+ mph) or premium electric jetboard (Awake RÄVIK 3, 35+ mph). If raw speed is your priority, surface-riding boards are your category. eFoils can be fast, but their sweet spot is 15–25 mph.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 "It's for my family / rental business"

Best match: eFoil with stable platform accessory (Lift Blowfish) for families. Electric bodyboards for rental fleets (cheap, nearly indestructible, zero training needed). Jetboards for rental operations targeting experienced riders.

🌊 "I'm an experienced foiler wanting to surf waves solo"

Best match: Tow boogie. Nothing else gives you the pure wave-riding foil experience without needing a boat driver. If you already foil and live near the ocean, this is the tool that changes everything.

🔧 "I want to build something with my own hands"

Best match: DIY eFoil ($2–4.5K) or DIY tow boogie ($750–$1.2K). Both have thriving open-source communities with complete build guides, component lists, and thousands of builders who've done it before you. The FOIL.zone forum is your home base.

The DIY Option: Build Your Own

Here's what most "electric surfboard review" sites will never tell you: you can build one yourself for a fraction of the retail price, and it'll outperform most commercial boards.

The DIY electric hydrofoil movement started in 2016 when I (PacificMeister) open-sourced my build designs on YouTube and created the FOIL.zone forum. Since then, thousands of builders worldwide have constructed their own eFoils, and the community knowledge is now incredibly deep.

DIY eFoil: $2,000–$4,500

  • Motor + ESC: $350–$900 (Flipsky 65161, VESC controller)
  • Battery: $500–$1,500 (12S or 14S lithium-ion pack)
  • Board: $200–$600 (modified surfboard or custom EPS build)
  • Foil: $300–$1,000 (Gong, used Lift, or AliExpress)
  • Electronics: $100–$300 (remote, BMS, wiring)

⏱️ Build time: 40–80 hours over 2–4 weekends

Buying Guide: What to Look For

1. Battery Capacity (kWh)

This is the single most important spec and the one most buyers overlook. Battery capacity directly determines your ride time. Look for kilowatt-hours (kWh), not just amp-hours (Ah) — a "20Ah" battery at 48V (0.96 kWh) gives twice the ride time of a "20Ah" battery at 24V (0.48 kWh).

  • Budget tier: 1.0–1.5 kWh → 30–50 min ride time
  • Mid-range: 1.5–2.0 kWh → 60–90 min
  • Premium: 2.0–3.0 kWh → 90–120+ min

2. Motor Type

  • Jet drive (internal impeller): Safer (no exposed blades), quieter, easier maintenance. Slightly less efficient.
  • Propeller (external): More efficient, better top speed. Requires a prop guard and more careful handling.
  • Folding prop: Best of both worlds for eFoils — folds flat when not powered for zero drag during wave riding.

3. Build Quality & Materials

  • Board: Carbon fiber (lightest, stiffest) > fiberglass > plastic. Inflatable options exist (Flite AIR) for easy transport.
  • Mast/foil: Carbon or aluminum. Carbon is lighter and stiffer but more expensive.
  • Waterproofing: The #1 failure point. Look for IP68 ratings and positive reviews about longevity.

4. Spare Parts & Service

This is where cheap brands collapse. Ask yourself:

  • Can I order a replacement battery? What does it cost?
  • Is there a dealer near me for warranty service?
  • Will this company exist in 3 years?
  • Are spare foil wings, props, and controllers available?
⚠️ The battery replacement trap. A $6,000 eFoil with a $2,500 replacement battery costs $8,500 over 3 years. A $10,000 eFoil with a $1,200 battery costs $11,200. Factor in the total cost of ownership, not just the purchase price. Or build your own and replace batteries for $400–$800.

5. Weight & Portability

You have to carry this thing to the water. A 35 kg (77 lb) eFoil is a two-person lift. A 25 kg board with detachable components is manageable solo. If you're driving to a lake, measure your trunk. If you're flying to vacation spots, consider inflatable options.

What to Avoid

🚫 Sub-$2,500 "eFoils" from Amazon/AliExpress

These boards are cheap for a reason. Common issues:

  • Uncertified batteries — genuine fire risk with low-quality lithium cells
  • Poor waterproofing — electronics fail within months
  • Weak motors — can't get heavier riders up on foil
  • No spare parts — when something breaks (it will), you own a paperweight
  • No warranty support — good luck reaching customer service

If $2,000–$4,500 for a proven DIY build sounds better than $2,500 for a disposable Chinese board, start here.

🚫 Counterfeit Brand-Name Boards

Fake eFoils and foil equipment are a growing problem, especially on Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist. Scammers sell stolen photos of real products at too-good-to-be-true prices. Red flags: price 40%+ below retail, seller won't meet in person, no serial number, shipping from a different country than advertised.

🔍 Our used eFoil buying guide covers how to spot scams and verify authenticity.

🚫 Boards Without Battery Replacement Options

If you can't replace the battery, the board has a built-in expiration date. Every lithium battery degrades over time. If the manufacturer doesn't sell replacement packs (or won't exist in 2 years), you're buying a very expensive toy with a 2–4 year lifespan.
